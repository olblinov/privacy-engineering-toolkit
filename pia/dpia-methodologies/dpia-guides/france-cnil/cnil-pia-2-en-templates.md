# Foreword

> The methodology of the French Data Protection Authority (CNIL) comprises three guides: one setting out the approach, a second containing facts that could be used for formalizing the analysis and a third providing knowledge bases (a catalogue of controls aimed at complying with the legal requirements and treating the risks, and examples):

![](media/image4.png)

> These can be downloaded
>
> from the CNIL\'s website:
>
> [[https://www.cnil.fr/en/privacy-impact-assessments-cnil-publishes-its-pia-manual]{.underline}](https://www.cnil.fr/en/privacy-impact-assessments-cnil-publishes-its-pia-manual)
>
> Writing conventions for all of these documents:

- the term \"**privacy**\" is used as shorthand to refer to all fundamental rights and freedoms (particularly those mentioned in the [[\[GDPR\]]{.underline}](http://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX%3A32016R0679&from=EN), by Articles 7 and 8 of the [[\[EU Charter\]]{.underline}](http://eur-lex.europa.eu/LexUriServ/LexUriServ.do?uri=OJ%3AC%3A2010%3A083%3A0389%3A0403%3AEN%3APDF) and Article 1 of the [[\[DP-Act\]]{.underline}](https://www.cnil.fr/sites/default/files/typo/document/Act78-17VA.pdf): \"privacy, human identity, human rights and individual or public liberties\");

- the acronym \"**PIA** \" is used interchangeably to refer to Privacy Impact Assessment and Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA);

- wordings in square brackets (\[title\]) correspond to references.

> **[Attention]{.underline}: the templates presented in this guide constitute an aid to the implementation of the approach. It is entirely possible and even desirable to adapt them to each particular context.**
